🏙️ Tokyo (4+2 nights)

Best areas:
• Ueno / Yanaka – old Tokyo charm, quiet, near Ueno park.
• Asakusa (east side) – near river, fewer crowds.
• Avoid Shinjuku/Shibuya if you want peace.

Budget: hostel/guesthouse ₹2,800–4,500
Mid: Mitsui Garden Ueno ₹8,500–12,000
Premium: The Gate Hotel Kaminarimon ₹18,000+
Airbnb: Yanaka private room ₹5,000–7,000

💡 Pick near Yamanote line for easy access.

⛩️ Kyoto (4 nights)

Quiet areas:
• Ohara / Kurama – nature, onsen, away from crowds.
• Demachiyanagi / Kitayama – local, near Kamo river.
• Central but calm: Sanjo (not Kawaramachi).

Budget: Guesthouse Rakuza ₹3,200–5,000
Mid: Solaria Nishitetsu ₹9,000–13,000
Premium ryokan: Seikoro Inn ₹22,000+
Airbnb: Machiya in north Kyoto ₹7,500

🍢 Osaka (3 nights)

Stay near:
• Tennoji / Shinsekai (retro, less intense than Namba)
• Kitahama – riverside, boutique hotels.
⚠️ Avoid Dotonbori accommodation if noise bothers you.

Budget: Hotel Toyo ₹2,000–3,500
Mid: Hotel the Flag Shinsaibashi ₹7,000–10,000
Premium: Zentis Osaka ₹14,000

🍱 Must‑try dishes

• Okonomiyaki (Osaka) – savory pancake.
• Yudofu (Kyoto) – tofu hotpot, great for vegetarians.
• Soba / Udon – tempura sets.
• Matcha sweets in Uji / Kyoto.
• Depachika (basement food halls) for affordable meals.

🥢 Street food & local

Tokyo: Yanaka Ginza (croquettes, onigiri).
Kyoto: Nishiki Market (go early 9am).
Osaka: Shinsekai kushikatsu, Kuromon market.
🟢 Low‑crowd alternative: Tenjinbashisuji shotengai (longest shopping street).

🌱 Indian / veg options

• Most convenience stores have egg sandwiches, onigiri (seaweed rice).
• Govinda’s / Veggie cafes in Tokyo/Kyoto.
• Use “Shōjin ryōri” (Buddhist vegan) in Kyoto.
• Indian restaurants in Nishi‑Kasai (Tokyo) & near Osaka station.

🛒 Local & mindful shopping

Tokyo: Yanaka & Kappabashi (kitchen knives, ceramics). Electronics: Bic Camera Akihabara (less overwhelming than Yodobashi).
Kyoto: Handmade furoshiki, Kiyomizu-yaki pottery, local incense (Kyukyodo).
Osaka: Sennichimae Doguyasuji (kitchenware), Hankyu Umeda food floor.
🟢 Avoid Takeshita-dori crowds: try Shimokitazawa for vintage.

🇯🇵 Japan visa for Indians · step‑by‑step

Documents: Passport, photo 45x35mm, ITR/ bank statements (6m), leave letter, detailed itinerary, flight/hotel bookings, cover letter.

Process: Apply via VFS Japan. Fee approx ₹600 (visa fee) + VFS charges ~₹900. Processing 4–6 working days.

Rejection avoidance: Show strong ties to India, consistent itinerary, sufficient funds (min ₹1.5L). Never submit dummy tickets.

✅ Apply 4 weeks before departure. Use e‑Visa for tourism (single entry 15/30 days) — faster for Indian passport.

📶 SIM & WiFi

Pocket WiFi or eSIM (Airalo / Ubigi). Cheaper: Japan Wireless pickup at airport. SIM cards need unlocked phone.

💴 Cash vs Card

Carry ¥20,000–30,000 cash. 7‑Eleven ATMs accept international cards. Many small shops cash only. IC card (Suica) for trains/conbini.

🧳 Packing (May)

Light jacket, umbrella, walking shoes, modest clothes for temples. Power bank. Carry passport always (tax‑free).

🚫 Things to avoid

✖ Eating while walking. ✖ Loud phone calls on trains. ✖ Tipping. ✖ Visiting Arashiyama bamboo at noon — go 7am or alternative Adashino Nenbutsuji.
