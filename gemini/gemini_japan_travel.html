12-Day Nature & Culture Focused Journey (Indian Traveler Edition)

Welcome to your Personalized Japan Journey

Kon'nichiwa! This itinerary is specifically designed for travelers from India starting their trip on May 20th. May is the perfect "Green Season"—pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds compared to the Sakura rush.

Trip Strategy:

  • The 7 AM Rule: Beat the tour buses by starting early. You'll have temples to yourself.
  • Regional Focus: Tokyo (Vibrant) → Kawaguchiko (Nature) → Kyoto (Zen) → Osaka (Food).
  • Indian Palate: Tips on finding vegetarian food and handling the "No-Spice" gap.

Day 1: Arrival in Tokyo & Shinjuku Gyoen

▼

The "Soft Landing" Route

Flow: Land at Narita/Haneda → Limousine Bus to Hotel → Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden → Early Dinner.

Why: Shinjuku Gyoen is massive; even with people, it feels empty. Perfect for shaking off jet lag with nature.

Transport: Airport Limousine Bus (easiest with luggage).

Food: Ain Soph.Journey (Vegan/Vegetarian friendly) in Shinjuku.

The City Skyline Route

Flow: Hotel → Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building → Omoide Yokocho.

Why: Free views of the city without the crowds of Tokyo Skytree.

The Neon Lights Route

Flow: Shinjuku Station → Kabukicho → Don Quijote Shopping.

Why: Intense, loud, and quintessentially "Tokyo."

Day 2: Coastal Zen (Kamakura Day Trip)

▼

Hokokuji Bamboo Forest & Zen

Flow: Tokyo → Kamakura Station → Hokokuji Temple → Hiking Trail to Zeniarai Benzaiten.

Experience: Sip Matcha in a hidden bamboo grove (much quieter than Kyoto’s Arashiyama).

Transport: JR Yokosuka Line (60 mins).

Tip: Avoid the main "Komachi-dori" shopping street; take the back alleys.

The Great Buddha Path

Flow: Kotoku-in (Giant Buddha) → Hase-dera Temple → Yuigahama Beach.

Enoshima Island

Flow: Enoden Train → Enoshima Shrine → Sea Candle.

Day 3-4: Mt. Fuji Immersive Stay

▼

Transfer via Fuji Excursion Train or Highway Bus from Shinjuku.

Oishi Park & Lake Saiko

Activity: Cycle around the quieter north shore of Lake Kawaguchi. Visit the Saiko Iyashi-no-Sato Nenba (Traditional thatched village).

Why: Most tourists stay at the ropeway; Saiko is peaceful and offers stunning Fuji views.

Chureito Pagoda at Sunrise

Must arrive by 5:30 AM to beat the buses.

Fuji-Q Highland

High-adrenaline theme park with crowds.

Day 5-8: Kyoto - The Cultural Heart

▼

The "Silver" Path

Day 5: Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion) early morning → Philosopher's Path → Nanzen-ji hidden aqueduct.

Day 6: Arashiyama Saga-Toriimoto Preserved Street (avoiding the main bamboo forest bridge).

Day 7: Uji Day Trip - Green tea capital and Byodo-in Temple.

Temples & Gion

Kiyomizu-dera late afternoon + Gion walk at dusk.

Fushimi Inari & Kinkaku-ji

The iconic 10,000 gates and Golden Pavilion (Very crowded).

Day 9-11: Osaka & Nara

▼

Day 9: Nara Day Trip (Deer Park). Pro-tip: Walk past the main crowd to Nigatsu-do temple.

Day 10: Osaka Exploration (Food focus).

Minoo Park & Nakazakicho

Hike to a waterfall inside the city (Minoo) and explore the hipster/vintage cafe district (Nakazakicho).

Osaka Castle & Shinsekai

History mixed with retro 1920s vibes.

Dotonbori & Universal Studios

The flashing lights and Mario World (extremely busy).

Day 12: Return to Tokyo & Shopping

▼

Shinkansen back to Tokyo. Final souvenir shopping at Ginza Itoya (Stationery) or Don Quijote.

Recommended Accommodation

Tokyo: Shinjuku or Ueno

Why: Best connectivity for day trips and airports.

  • Budget: APA Hotels (₹6k - ₹8k)
  • Mid: Hotel Sunroute Plaza (₹12k - ₹15k)
  • Premium: Park Hyatt Tokyo

Kyoto: Near Sanjo or Gion

Why: Walkable to temples and local eateries.

  • Ryokan: Gion Hatanaka (Traditional Experience)
  • Modern: Cross Hotel Kyoto (₹15k+)
  • Airbnb: Search for "Machiya" (Traditional townhouse)

Food Guide for Indians

Dietary Tips

  • Vegetarianism: Japan uses Dashi (fish stock) in everything. Carry "Dietary Requirement" cards in Japanese.
  • Indian Food: You'll find great curry houses (like Coco Ichibanya with veg options) and authentic Indian spots in Nishi-Kasai (Tokyo).

Must Try:

Teppanyaki

Grilled vegetables and paneer-like tofu or high-grade Wagyu.

Tempura

Fried veggies/seafood. Very light and crisp.

Matcha Everything

Especially in Uji and Kyoto.

Visa for Indian Citizens

Updated 2026

  1. Type: Short-term Tourist Visa.
  2. e-Visa: Indian passport holders can now apply for an e-Visa online if residing in India.
  3. Documents:
    • Valid Passport
    • Photo (2x2 inches, white background)
    • ITR (Last 3 years)
    • Bank Statement (Last 6 months, balance > ₹3-4 Lakhs)
    • Cover Letter & Itinerary
  4. Processing: 5-7 working days via VFS Global.

Essential Hacks

  • Suica Card: Add it to your Apple/Google Wallet. Tap and go for all trains and vending machines.
  • Luggage Forwarding (Takkyubin): Send your bags from Tokyo to Kyoto for ₹1,500. Travel light on the bullet train!
  • Trash: There are no public dustbins. Carry a small bag to keep your trash until you reach your hotel.

Etiquette

  • No talking on phones in trains.
  • No tipping! It's considered rude.
  • Stand on the correct side of the escalator (Left in Tokyo, Right in Osaka).
